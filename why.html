Why would anyone want to do this?

Perhaps the better question is, given the opportunity, why wouldn't anyone want to do this? There are so many ways to enjoy the Camino experience; you may walk, you may bicycle, you may traverse on horseback, and so on and so forth. There is no right or wrong way to undertake your journey to the end of the world. I began walking from Astorga and ended in Finisterre, an approximate 217 miles. I walked alone because, like many others on the trail, I needed to refocus and get back in touch with myself. There's nothing quite like walking day after day to clear one's mind. After day three, it finally hit me: when I wake up tomorrow, the only thing I have to do, the only thing I have to concentrate on, is walking. I had everything I needed with me, strapped to my back. It was an immeasurably liberating feeling. In a way, I would say that my Camino experience wound up being a spiritual one, even though my reasons for wanting to embark upon this journey had nothing to do with religious beliefs. To this day, I find myself looking back and thinking, "Wow, I did that. I was alone and yet I was never lonely." I made friends from all over the world, ate foods I'd never heard of, and lived a life (however temporarily) beyond my wildest dreams.

Thank you for taking the time to visit my site. I have included a list of helpful links in case you feel inclined to research the possibility of your own Camino. Wherever your path leads, I wish you a very "Buen Camino."
